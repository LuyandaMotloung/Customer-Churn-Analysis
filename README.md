# Customer-Churn-Analysis
# Project Overview
This project analyses customer data from a telecommunications company to identify key factors that contribute to churn, assess impact on revenue and provide strategies/recommendations for customer retention. Customer churn is a major challenge for telecommunication companies as it directly affects revenue, understanding patterns in customer behavior can help companies reduce customer loss and improve retention.

# Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel: Data cleaning, standardisation and pivot tables
- Power BI: DAX measures, interactive dashboard and KPIs for data visualisation

# Dataset
- Source: Kaggle - Telco Customer Churn Dataset
- Size: 7043 records, 33 columns

# Key Insights
1. The company has a 27% churn rate.
2. Approximately 31% of monthly revenue is lost due to customer churn.
3. Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate.
4. The highest churn occurs within the first 12 months, even though the average tenure for leaving customers is 18 months. This indicates a critical early stage drop off that requires early retention strategies.
5. Competitive market offerings are the key contributing factor to customer churn.

# Recommendations
1. Implement a structured onboarding program for new customers, this ensures customers quickly understand the value from the service, improves engagement and reduces the likelihood of early churn.
2. Offer targeted discounts or loyalty rewards to customers on month-to-month contracts to encourage transition to longer term contracts.
3. Target high-value customers with personalised retention offers such as contract upgrades, improved customer service and discounts.
4. Benchmark pricing and service offerings against competitors regularly to strengthen market competitiveness.
